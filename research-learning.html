Distributed learning

← Back to Research interests

Distributed learning studies how to train models when data and computation are spread across devices or agents. This includes decentralized learning, federated learning, and learning with communication constraints, with an emphasis on reliability, privacy, and performance analysis.

What this field is about

  • Learning with distributed data (no single machine sees all data).
  • Communication-efficient training: fewer messages, less bandwidth, robust to dropouts.
  • Privacy-aware learning and trustworthy model updates.

Illustrations

Federated learning

Devices keep data locally and send model updates for aggregation (privacy-friendly collaboration).

Learning over graphs

Local learning plus neighbor messages enables robust performance in decentralized settings.

Typical applications

  • Edge AI, collaborative perception, and smart-city sensing.
  • Networked robotics and autonomous systems with distributed intelligence.
  • Learning-based control and optimization in cyber-physical systems.
